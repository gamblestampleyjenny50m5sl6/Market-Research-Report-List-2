<p><p>Legumes and nuts are rich sources of dietary fibers, including cellulose, pectin, xylan, mannose, and others. Cellulose is a plant-based fiber that aids digestion and promotes gut health. Pectin is a soluble fiber that can help regulate blood sugar levels and lower cholesterol. Xylan is a prebiotic fiber that supports the growth of healthy gut bacteria. Mannose is a type of sugar that may help improve immune function. These different types of dietary fibers in legumes and nuts provide various health benefits and contribute to overall well-being.</p></p>
