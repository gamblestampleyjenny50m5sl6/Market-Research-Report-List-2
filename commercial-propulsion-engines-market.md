<p><p>Commercial propulsion engines are categorized into two main types based on their rotational speeds: medium speed and high speed engines. Medium speed engines typically operate within the range of 300-1000 RPM and are commonly used in large vessels such as cargo ships and ferries. On the other hand, high speed engines operate at speeds above 1000 RPM and are commonly used in smaller vessels or applications that require quicker acceleration. Both types of engines play a crucial role in powering commercial vehicles and marine vessels.</p></p>
