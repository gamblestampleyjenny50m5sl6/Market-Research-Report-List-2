<p><p>Solid ceramic end mills are categorized into three main types based on the materials used in their construction - iron-based, nickel-based, and cobalt-based. Iron-based ceramic end mills are known for their high hardness and wear resistance, making them suitable for machining hardened steels. Nickel-based ceramic end mills offer good thermal stability and are often used for high-speed cutting applications. Cobalt-based ceramic end mills exhibit excellent resistance to high temperatures and are commonly used in machining superalloys and other heat-resistant materials.</p></p>
