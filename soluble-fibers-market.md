<p><p>Soluble fibers are classified into various types in the market: inulin, oligosaccharides, resistant starch, resistant maltodextrin, polydextrose, beta-glucan, and others. Inulin is a fiber sourced from plants, while oligosaccharides are short-chain carbohydrates. Resistant starch and resistant maltodextrin resist digestion in the small intestine. Polydextrose is a synthetic soluble fiber, and beta-glucan is found in oats and barley. Other types of soluble fibers include pectin, psyllium, and guar gum. Each type offers unique health benefits and functional properties in food products.</p></p>
